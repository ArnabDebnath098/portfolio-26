## Hey there!

I recently took part in a UX Design hackathon, and the task was to design a checkout experience for an e-commerce platform. I've been feeling underwhelmed by the checkout processes on sites like Amazon and Flipkart — so I decided to let my creative juices flow and cook up something special. A checkout journey that's not just functional but downright delightful.

I was the only solo designer competing against teams of talented designers. And I won.

---

## The Problem

In today's digital landscape, we encounter numerous e-commerce platforms offering diverse products tailored to various user demographics. Each platform has its unique approach to meeting user needs. For me, online shopping has become heavily reliant on trust. Sometimes, there are issues like receiving the wrong product, poor quality items, or dealing with complex return processes.

The issues I faced as a user:

- **Complex cart page** with an overload of product information
- **Difficult navigation** between sections — cart, address, payment, review
- **Lack of trust signals** — no reassurance when handing over money
- **Hidden offers section** — discounts buried behind unclear entry points
- **Poorly optimized payment pages** — too many options, no guidance

> Before I start designing, I'm assuming the user has already put some items in their cart.

---

## The Goal

Create a delightful experience that improves conversion rate and lets users complete the checkout process with confidence. No stress, no worries — just pure, hassle-free shopping bliss. Every click feels like a high-five and every step brings us closer to happy customers hitting that "buy" button with confidence.

---

## Cart Page

The cart is where users make their final decisions. Instead of overwhelming them, I designed it as a calm, focused space — with address display alongside delivery times, Open Box Delivery for trust, smart upselling, upfront offers and coupons, a transparent order summary, and delivery instructions.

One of the issues I encounter on e-commerce platforms is accidentally selecting the wrong address during checkout. Since I spend a lot of time on the cart page reviewing details, it would be helpful to have the address displayed. Also, with items having different delivery times, I find it cumbersome to scroll through the list. Therefore, I thought of incorporating delivery times alongside the address.

To build user confidence and prevent fraud, Open Box Delivery ensures users receive their items without defects or errors, eliminating the hassle of returns and refunds.

In the cart section, upselling can boost average order value and improve the shopping experience. Additionally, users may be reminded of related items they forgot to purchase.

Customers feel thrilled to receive discounts or cashback on their purchases, as it gives them a sense of saving money. Therefore, it's crucial to present offers upfront and clearly, allowing users to apply coupons easily without searching extensively.

Hidden charges and unclear bill breakdowns can leave users confused about the exact costs. Providing a clear and transparent bill breakdown is essential to instill confidence and trust in the platform.

Working individuals often need to communicate specific delivery instructions to ensure proper receipt of their packages. By allowing users to provide these instructions upfront on the platform, it simplifies the process for both the delivery partner and the customer.

A friendly reminder to double-check your address and cart items to prevent cancellations.

## Cart Page Details

### Open Box Delivery

Open Box Delivery allows customers to inspect their products upon delivery, ensuring confidence and top-notch quality by enabling them to verify the condition before accepting.

### Remove Items from Cart

Swipe cards to either delete items or save them for later, to purchase whenever you're ready.

### Offers & Coupons

To maintain user excitement and encourage conversions, offers CTAs are placed upfront, allowing users to easily navigate to the offer section and apply discounts they might have forgotten.

## Cart Item Cards

Users can seamlessly switch between different versions of products with just a click, facilitating quick comparison and selection. The card handles multiple states:

- **Default state** — Product image, name, price, delivery time, rating, and quantity controls
- **Urgency state** — "Hurry! Only 10 pairs left in stock. Don't miss out!" or "Hurry! Sale ends in 02:58 min"
- **Out of stock state** — "Out of stock. Slide to remove" with clear visual treatment

![Cart Item Cards — States and Variants](/images/case-studies/streamlining-checkout/section-3.png)

## Cart Confirm & Review Order

After adding your favorite items to your cart, if you have an account with a saved address, you can easily select it or add a new one or edit the current address seamlessly.

After reviewing your order, the "Review Order" option allows you to double-check and avoid any mistakes before confirming your purchase — delivery details, itemized pricing, discount breakdown, shipping charges, and total amount all in one clear summary.

![Address Selection & Order Review](/images/case-studies/streamlining-checkout/section-4.png)

## Guest Checkout

For new users without an account:

### Phone & Name

To facilitate order notifications and streamline communication with the delivery partner, customer's phone number and name is required.

### OTP Verification

Implementing OTP verification helps ensure user authenticity, reducing delivery failures caused by incorrect phone numbers. This minimizes returns but also saves on delivery costs, optimizing operational efficiency.

### Address Entry

To simplify address entry, users can utilize their current location for accurate identification. If ordering for someone else, they can toggle to manually enter every detail, including pincode, exact address, landmark, etc., for better delivery precision.

### Review Order

After reviewing everything, users can confirm all details at once in a single place before proceeding to payment.

![Guest Checkout — OTP, Address Entry, Review](/images/case-studies/streamlining-checkout/section-5.png)

## Select Payment Method

This is where trust matters most. Users are about to commit their money.

Displaying the last-used payment methods upfront ensures a seamless payment experience, sparing users the need to remember and navigate to off-set.

When the total due is high, subtly promoting EMI options below payment methods ensures users aren't forced to availability, making after it easy and inviting. The page is simple with only a credit card like card and few confirmation fields in reach.

The payment page groups all options clearly — UPI apps with familiar branding, credit and debit cards with auto-formatting, wallets, pay-later options, and COD. Coupons can be applied directly, offer-eligible methods surface with savings tags, and the summary updates in real-time.

For processes with smart wallets, Gpay, PhonePe, or wallets and mobile payments, the experience is designed to be seamless. The pay-now button always shows the exact amount with a clear confirmation.

## Order Tracking

After successful payment, users see a satisfying confirmation with order details, delivery timeline, and a clear tracking interface. The post-payment screen isn't just a receipt — it's a celebration and a reassurance that everything went through correctly.

---

## Got New Ideas — Code to Get Address

During the design process, I explored the idea of using delivery partner codes as an alternative to traditional addresses. Instead of typing full addresses, users could generate a unique code that delivery partners scan or enter — simplifying the last-mile delivery problem entirely.

## Things I Designed

Throughout this hackathon, I designed the complete checkout system from scratch:

- **Cart page** with inline editing, upselling, and transparent pricing
- **Product card system** with multiple states (default, urgency, out of stock)
- **Address selection** flow for both returning and new users
- **Guest checkout** with OTP verification and location-based address
- **Payment method selection** with intelligent ranking and offer integration
- **Order confirmation** and tracking experience
- **Design system components** — buttons, cards, inputs, modals, and interaction patterns

## That's a Wrap

This hackathon pushed me to rethink patterns I'd taken for granted. Competing solo against teams made every design decision count — there was no room for filler screens or half-baked flows.

The judges highlighted three things: the trust-building approach to payment UX, the inline editing cart experience, and the overall polish of the end-to-end flow.

> **The lesson:** A great checkout doesn't ask users to trust the platform — it earns trust at every interaction. Every visible price breakdown, every familiar payment logo, every smooth transition whispers: *"You're in good hands."*
