## TL;DR

UPI mandates (autopay) let merchants block or debit recurring amounts from a user's bank account. But the existing flow was confusing — users didn't understand what they were authorizing, how much would be debited, or how to revoke it. I redesigned the mandate creation and confirmation experience across desktop and mobile, making the process transparent, trustworthy, and simple.

**Problem:** Users abandoned mandate setup because the flow felt opaque and risky
**Scope:** Desktop web, Mobile web, Android & iOS — checkout UX

---

## Context

BASBA (Block Amount and Send Back on Approval) is a UPI-based payment flow where the premium amount is blocked via a one-time mandate and only debited after merchant confirmation. This is critical for industries like insurance, subscriptions, and recurring services where the final amount may differ from the initial authorization.

The challenge: mandate flows involve multiple parties (merchant, payment aggregator, UPI app, bank) and require users to understand concepts like "mandate amount vs premium amount" and "blocking vs debiting" — financial concepts most users have never encountered.

> **The core tension:** How do you make a complex multi-party financial authorization feel as simple as a regular payment?

## Approach

The redesign focused on three principles:

1. **Transparency first** — Show exactly what's being authorized: the premium amount, the mandate ceiling, the end date, and the frequency. No hidden terms.

2. **Progressive disclosure** — Don't overwhelm users with all details upfront. Surface the essentials (amount, app, action) and let curious users dig deeper via "Know More" links.

3. **Trust through familiarity** — Use familiar UPI app logos, bank names, and payment patterns so the mandate flow feels like a natural extension of a regular UPI payment.

---

## Key Decisions

- **Split-panel desktop layout:** Payment methods on the left, detailed flow on the right — mirrors the mental model of "choose then configure"
- **QR + UPI ID dual path:** Desktop users get both scan-to-pay and direct UPI ID entry, reducing friction for different user preferences
- **Mandate details card:** A clear summary showing Premium Amount, Mandate Amount, End Date, and Frequency — all visible before the user taps "Proceed to Pay"
- **Multi-app support:** CRED, PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, BHIM — users see their preferred app upfront with familiar branding

---

## Impact

- Reduced mandate setup abandonment
- Improved user trust through transparent amount disclosure
- Consistent experience across 500+ merchant integrations
