## Overview

Most people investing today aren't financial experts — they're curious, time-poor, and managing money across **4–6 disconnected apps** with no real sense of where they stand. The tools exist, but the clarity doesn't.

**Fynra** is a freelance project I designed over 1 month: a single intelligent dashboard where users track all their assets, see growth over time, and get plain-language AI recommendations based on their goals and risk profile — no finance degree required.

**Problem:** Fragmented tools and jargon-heavy interfaces leave today's retail investors guessing instead of growing
**Scope:** Mobile-first app — consumer fintech, built for a new generation of first-generation retail investors

---

## Problem

India's retail investor base grew from **40M to 150M** demat accounts between 2020 and 2025. Most of these investors are first-generation — managing portfolios across multiple brokers (Zerodha, Groww), mutual fund platforms (Coin, Kuvera), crypto exchanges, and offline assets like gold and real estate.

Nobody has a complete picture. Decisions get made in silos — selling mutual funds while ignoring underperforming stocks, over-allocating to crypto without seeing total equity exposure.

> The problem wasn't that people weren't investing. It was that **they had no idea what they actually owned**.

When I spoke to 15 retail investors (age 22–35, portfolio ₹50K–₹20L), the same frustrations came up:

- **No unified view** — stocks on one app, mutual funds on another, FDs in a bank app. No single screen showing the full picture
- **Manual growth tracking** — users screenshot portfolio values or maintain spreadsheets. Most give up within months
- **No goal-based structure** — investing without purpose. No way to know if your SIP will actually reach your house down payment target
- **Generic or no recommendations** — apps either show raw data with no guidance, or push products with no relevance to who you are
- **Jargon overload** — XIRR, expense ratio, alpha. New investors want one answer: *"Am I on track? What should I do next?"*

Two types of users. Two different needs.

**The Active Tracker** — Checks their portfolio frequently, wants depth and detail, open to recommendations if they're grounded in their actual data.

**The Set-and-Forget Investor** — Runs SIPs, rarely opens finance apps. Needs reassurance over complexity — just show them they're on track.

> Fynra had to work for both — **calm enough for the passive investor, rich enough for the active one**.

---

## Approach

Before opening Figma, the first decision was **what to build and what not to**.

We scoped Fynra around four pillars that mapped directly to the pain points:

- **Unified portfolio view** — every asset in one place, live valuation across all classes
- **Automatic growth tracking** — portfolio history that builds itself, no manual logging
- **Goal-based investing** — every investment tied to a real-world outcome with progress tracking
- **Personalised AI suggestions** — recommendations based on the user's risk profile, existing holdings, and stated goals

**What we explicitly didn't build yet:** in-app execution (buy/sell), social features, tax harvesting calculators. Scope discipline kept the experience focused.

**Why mobile-first:** Gen Z checks portfolios in short bursts — the 90-second check-in, not the 30-minute deep dive. A desktop-first experience would solve the wrong problem.

> The constraint we kept coming back to: **this app should feel calmer than the market**. Finance apps are anxiety-inducing by design. Fynra had to feel like a trusted advisor, not a Bloomberg terminal.

## Onboarding

**Pain point:** Fintech apps often ask for too much too soon — forms, KYC, permissions — before the user has any reason to trust them. The drop-off happens before they've seen a single number.

Fynra's onboarding is **three screens**. Brand positioning first, phone number authentication second, OTP verification third. No long forms. No KYC walls upfront. The user is in the app before they've had a chance to second-guess the decision.

## Dashboard

**Pain point:** Most portfolio apps show too much — tickers, alerts, red numbers everywhere. Users open the app, feel anxious, and close it without acting.

The home screen answers **three questions** every investor has the moment they open the app: how much do I have, where is it, and how is it doing? Everything above the fold is dedicated to those three answers.

Total portfolio value, allocation breakdown by asset class, and performance trend — nothing else. The calm visual language is intentional: this should feel like checking in with someone you trust, not watching the market panic.

## Stock Details

**Pain point:** Existing stock detail pages overwhelm users with every metric available. New investors don't know what to look at, experienced ones have to dig for what they need.

The stock detail view has **three states designed with intent**. The default overview shows price, current value, and performance — the numbers needed for a quick decision. The expanded view adds a historical chart with a range selector for users who want more context. The action state surfaces watchlist and buy/exit prompts only when the user signals intent — never cluttering the default experience.

## Goal-Based Investing

**Pain point:** People invest without knowing why. A SIP running for 3 years with no target attached feels meaningless — users can't tell if they're ahead or behind because they never defined what "on track" looks like.

Fynra ties every investment to a **real-world outcome**. Users create goals — a house, retirement, an emergency fund — set a target amount and deadline, and the app calculates the monthly SIP needed to get there. From then on, every portfolio view has context: you're not just tracking returns, you're tracking progress toward something that matters.

## AI Assistant

**Pain point:** Users know they should be doing something with their money — but don't know what. Generic advice doesn't help. They need answers grounded in their actual situation, not broad market commentary.

The AI chat turns Fynra into a **financial advisor in your pocket**. Users ask plain-language questions — "Should I sell my HDFC Bank shares?", "Am I diversified enough?", "How much do I need to invest to retire at 50?" — and get answers that reference their actual portfolio, goals, and risk profile.

The flow is designed around three natural states: an empty screen with prompt suggestions to lower the barrier to asking, the user's query in progress, and the AI response with reasoning and a concrete recommendation.

## Reflection

Two months. One designer. A product that went from problem statement to a complete, handover-ready design system — onboarding, portfolio, stock details, goal tracking, news, and AI chat — all delivered as an annotated Figma file with components, flows, and edge cases documented.

The founders built a functional prototype from the designs and put it in front of real users. The feedback sessions validated the core hypothesis: **a unified, calm, AI-grounded experience is exactly what first-generation retail investors are missing**.

- **Unified view works** — users immediately understood their total picture in a way they couldn't before
- **Goals create engagement** — tying investments to outcomes made the data feel personal, not abstract
- **AI reduces anxiety** — getting a plain-language answer grounded in their actual portfolio built more trust than any generic recommendation engine

> My job was to take a messy whiteboard to a testable product in two months. The design foundation is there — clear information hierarchy, a calm visual language, flows that respect how Gen Z actually thinks about money. What gets built from here is up to the team. What I handed over was something they could confidently show to investors and users alike.
