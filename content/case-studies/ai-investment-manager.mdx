## TL;DR

Most investors juggle 4–6 apps to track their money — one for stocks, one for mutual funds, a spreadsheet for real estate, a DeFi wallet for crypto. **Wealth Lens** brings everything into a single dashboard where users can add all their assets, track investment history and growth, and receive AI-suggested investment recommendations based on their goals, risk appetite, and market conditions.

**Problem:** Fragmented investment tracking leads to poor financial decisions and missed opportunities
**Scope:** Mobile-first responsive web app — consumer fintech

---

## Context

India's retail investor base has exploded — from 40M demat accounts in 2020 to over 150M in 2025. Yet most of these investors are first-generation, managing portfolios across multiple brokers (Zerodha, Groww, Angel One), mutual fund platforms (Coin, Kuvera), crypto exchanges (WazirX, CoinSwitch), and offline assets like real estate and gold.

The result: nobody has a complete picture of their wealth. Decisions are made in silos — selling mutual funds while ignoring underperforming stocks, or over-allocating to crypto without seeing total equity exposure. Existing tools either focus on one asset class or require tedious manual entry.

> **The core question:** How do you give everyday investors a single, intelligent view of everything they own — and help them make better decisions without requiring a finance degree?

---

## Defining the Problem

I interviewed 15 retail investors (age 24–40, portfolio size ₹2L–₹50L) across experience levels. The findings were consistent:

### Key Pain Points

- **No single source of truth** — Investors check 3–5 apps daily. Nobody knows their actual net worth at any given moment.
- **Growth tracking is manual** — Users screenshot portfolio values or maintain spreadsheets to track performance over time. Most give up after a few months.
- **Decision paralysis** — "Should I invest more in this fund or diversify?" Without holistic context, every decision feels like guesswork.
- **Jargon overload** — Terms like Sharpe ratio, CAGR, expense ratio, and alpha intimidate new investors. They want simple answers: "Am I doing well? What should I do next?"
- **No actionable insights** — Apps show data but don't tell you what to do with it. A 12% return sounds good until you realize the benchmark did 18%.

## Design Principles

1. **Consolidate, don't complicate** — Adding an asset should take under 30 seconds. Auto-sync where possible, manual add as fallback.
2. **Insights over information** — Don't just show numbers. Tell the user what the numbers mean and what to do about it.
3. **Progressive intelligence** — Start simple (total value, basic growth). Unlock deeper AI insights as the user adds more data and sets goals.
4. **Trust through transparency** — Every AI suggestion must show its reasoning. "Why is it recommending this?" should always be answerable.

## Core Flows

### 1. Onboarding & Asset Addition

Getting users to add their first assets is the critical activation moment:

1. **Quick risk profile** — 5-question quiz (investment horizon, risk comfort, income stability) that takes under 60 seconds
2. **Auto-sync brokers** — Connect Zerodha, Groww, Angel One via OAuth. Stocks and mutual funds import automatically
3. **Manual asset entry** — For real estate, gold, FDs, and crypto. Guided forms with smart defaults (e.g., current gold rate auto-filled)
4. **First dashboard moment** — Once 2+ assets are added, the dashboard lights up with net worth, allocation breakdown, and the first AI insight

> Design decision: I show the dashboard with partial data rather than gating it behind "add all assets." Seeing value immediately motivates users to add more.

### 2. Dashboard & Portfolio Overview

The dashboard is the daily landing screen. It answers three questions at a glance:

- **"How much do I have?"** — Net worth prominently displayed with daily/weekly/monthly change
- **"Where is my money?"** — Asset allocation donut chart with drill-down by category
- **"Am I doing well?"** — Portfolio performance vs. benchmark (Nifty 50, S&P 500) with simple up/down indicators

Key interactions:
- Tap any segment of the allocation chart to see individual holdings
- Swipe between time ranges (1W, 1M, 3M, 1Y, All) on the growth chart
- Pull to refresh for latest prices

### 3. Investment History & Growth Tracking

The history section automatically tracks portfolio value over time — no manual logging needed:

- **Net worth timeline** — Area chart showing total portfolio value with key events marked (large deposits, market crashes, rebalances)
- **Asset-wise performance** — Compare how each asset class performed over the same period
- **Holding period returns** — For each investment, show actual returns vs. what you'd have earned in a fixed deposit (opportunity cost framing)
- **Monthly snapshots** — Auto-generated monthly summary: what grew, what fell, net change, new investments made

### 4. AI Investment Advisor

The centerpiece feature. The AI advisor analyzes the user's complete portfolio and provides:

**Portfolio Health Score (0–100)**
A single number that captures diversification, risk-adjusted returns, goal alignment, and cost efficiency. The score breaks down into:
- Diversification (are you over-concentrated?)
- Risk alignment (does your portfolio match your stated risk appetite?)
- Cost efficiency (are you paying high expense ratios unnecessarily?)
- Goal progress (are you on track for your financial goals?)

**Actionable Suggestions**
Each suggestion is a card with:
- What to do ("Invest ₹5,000 in Nifty 50 Index Fund")
- Why ("Your large-cap allocation is 15% below your target. This fund has the lowest expense ratio at 0.1%")
- Expected impact ("This would improve your diversification score from 62 to 71")
- One-tap action (deep link to broker app or in-app investment)

**Types of suggestions:**
- **Invest** — Where to put new money based on current allocation gaps
- **Rebalance** — Sell overweight positions, buy underweight ones
- **Exit** — Flag consistently underperforming holdings with better alternatives
- **Protect** — Emergency fund adequacy, insurance gaps, over-exposure to volatile assets

**Goal-Based Planning**
Users set financial goals (retirement at 50, buy house in 5 years, ₹10L emergency fund). The AI:
- Calculates required monthly SIP to reach each goal
- Shows current progress as a percentage
- Adjusts recommendations when the user falls behind or gets ahead
- Sends gentle nudges ("You're ₹3,000 short on this month's retirement contribution")

## Key Design Decisions

### Health Score Over Raw Metrics

New investors don't understand Sharpe ratios. A single 0–100 score with color coding (red/yellow/green) is immediately intuitive. Power users can expand to see the underlying metrics, but the score is the hook.

### Explanation-First AI

Every AI suggestion shows its reasoning in plain language. "Invest in X" is not enough — "Invest in X because your portfolio is 70% in one sector, and this fund gives you exposure to healthcare which you currently have 0% in" builds trust and educates.

### Passive Tracking Over Active Logging

The #1 reason portfolio trackers are abandoned is the maintenance burden. Wealth Lens tracks value automatically via broker sync and live pricing. The user's job is just to add assets once — the app does the rest.

### Nudge, Don't Nag

Financial apps that send daily notifications get muted. Wealth Lens sends a maximum of 2 notifications per week:
- Weekly portfolio summary (Sunday evening)
- Urgent alerts only (significant market events affecting their holdings, goal deadlines approaching)

## Visual Design

The visual language conveys **trust, clarity, and calm** — critical for a finance app:

- **Color palette**: Deep navy primary (#1a1f36), white surfaces, green for gains, warm red for losses. Accent: electric blue (#3366FF) for CTAs and AI elements
- **Typography**: Clean geometric sans-serif. Tabular numbers for financial data. Large, confident net worth display
- **Charts**: Minimal, no grid lines. Smooth curves with gradient fills. Interactive but not overwhelming
- **AI elements**: Subtle glow/aurora effect on AI suggestion cards to differentiate them from static data
- **Empty states**: Encouraging, not clinical. "Your portfolio is waiting" with illustration, not "No data found"

## Interaction Details

### Smart Asset Detection

When a user connects a broker, the app doesn't just import current holdings — it reconstructs transaction history to show accurate cost basis and holding period returns. This means:
- True XIRR calculations, not just point-to-point returns
- Tax-lot tracking for capital gains estimation
- Dividend reinvestment properly accounted for

### Real-Time Portfolio Simulation

Before acting on a suggestion, users can simulate the impact:
- "If I invest ₹10,000 in this fund, how does my allocation change?"
- Visual before/after of the allocation donut
- Updated health score preview
- Projected growth with the new investment added

### Market Context Cards

When markets move significantly, context cards appear on the dashboard:
- "Nifty fell 3% today. Your portfolio impact: -₹12,400 (1.2%)"
- "Your tech stocks fell more than the market. AI suggests: hold — fundamentals unchanged"
- Prevents panic selling by framing losses in portfolio context rather than absolute numbers

## Outcome & Impact

Usability tested with 10 participants (mix of new and experienced investors):

- **Onboarding completion**: 90% added 3+ assets within 5 minutes
- **Dashboard comprehension**: 100% could accurately state their net worth and top allocation within 10 seconds
- **AI trust score**: 4.2/5 — users rated AI suggestions as "trustworthy" when reasoning was shown (vs. 2.8/5 without reasoning in A/B test)
- **Health Score engagement**: 8/10 users immediately tried to understand how to improve their score
- **Weekly retention intent**: 9/10 said they would check the app at least weekly (vs. 4/10 for their current tracking method)

> "This is the first app that actually tells me what to do instead of just showing me numbers I don't understand." — Test participant (age 27, 2 years investing experience)

## Reflection

### What Worked

- **The Health Score** was the breakout feature — users immediately latched onto it as a gamified motivation to improve their portfolio
- **Explanation-first AI** dramatically increased trust. The A/B test proved reasoning isn't optional
- **Auto-sync eliminated the logging burden** that kills most portfolio trackers

### What I'd Do Differently

- **Tax optimization suggestions** — Users asked about tax harvesting and LTCG/STCG implications. This was out of scope but clearly valuable
- **Social benchmarking** — "How does my portfolio compare to people my age?" was a common request. Needs careful privacy design
- **Spending integration** — Connecting bank accounts to track savings rate alongside investments would complete the financial picture

### What's Next

- Build a functional prototype with mock portfolio data and real market pricing APIs
- Explore partnerships with discount brokers for seamless in-app investing
- Add multi-currency support for NRI investors
- Design a "Family Portfolio" view for household-level wealth tracking
